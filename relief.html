Disaster Relief Library Wireframes
This is a wireframe prototype for Disaster Relief Library; A Resource Sharing App for Emergency Situations.

Disaster Relief Library is a medium for those with a surplus of equipment, resources or skills can safely share with those who are in need.

Through the use of a map where resources are available, this app aims to remove the need for centralized resource centers (such as grocery stores) making perhaps critical resources more available to people faster in disaster situations.

These wireframes were created using Figma.

Map


The Map is the landing page of App. Here you can see what items are being offered, requested and where areas of danger are. When clicking on an item, you can see more details and find paths to that item.

Map Menus


The Top icons allow you to pull down an Announcements menu, which gives you information on the current situation, and extend a search bar, which lets you search the area for whatever you may be looking for.

Login


A login page will appear if you open the app and you are not already logged in. There is a option for creating an account too.

Requests


The requests section is navigated to through the Nav Bar. It lists all items that are needed in your area. When selecting an item, you can see more details about the item and how much Karma you’d recieve by trading it.

Requests


Very similar to the requests page, this page is made different by its colour theme. This is where a user can offer their goods and services.

Profile


The Profile tab is a user manages their Profile, Karma, and requests.

Profile - Offers


Here you can see what you are offereing to your community and how you make a new posts. You can also see if you’ve received offers (the red circle with the number) and when selecting an item you can manage your requests.

Profile - Requests


Very similar to the Offers page, only with your requests.

Messages


Finally the messages page. The final option on the Nav bar allows you to arrange delivery, pick up and to contact other people using the App.
